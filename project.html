This page presents my analysis of the Swachh Bharat Mission (SBM) and its correlation with Swachh Survekshan data.

Part I: Introduction

Objective: This project analyzes the Swachh Bharat Mission (SBM), focusing on Swachh Survekshan rankings, state performance, and factors influencing SBM outcomes across various states in India.

Data: Swachh Survekshan Reports, SBM data from Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs, and Census data. Swachh Bharat Mission Website

Part II: State-wise Rankings in Swachh Survekshan

Objective: This section visualizes state-wise rankings in Swachh Survekshan over the years, with a focus on key performing states.

Data Source: Swachh Survekshan Rankings (2016-2023)


Analysis: The chart highlights the top-performing states such as Indore, Surat, and Ambikapur, alongside regions lagging behind. Patterns suggest a strong link between political leadership, funding, and Swachh Bharat outcomes.

Part III: Relationship Between Sanitation and Health Indicators

Objective: Explore the relationship between sanitation outcomes in Swachh Bharat and key health indicators, such as child mortality, stunting, and maternal health.


Interpretation: A negative correlation between child malnutrition and access to improved sanitation is observed. States with higher sanitation coverage report lower incidences of stunting and child mortality.

Part IV: Mapping Sanitation Coverage Across India

Objective: Analyze the spatial distribution of sanitation infrastructure in India using choropleth mapping techniques.


Insight: States like Himachal Pradesh and Kerala show high sanitation coverage, while states such as Uttar Pradesh and Bihar show room for improvement in infrastructure and access to sanitation.

Part V: SBM Achievements and Challenges

Objective: Review the progress of Swachh Bharat Mission in terms of coverage, behavior change, and challenges faced in rural and urban areas.


Challenges: Despite impressive progress in terms of coverage, challenges persist in sustaining behavior change, maintaining cleanliness, and ensuring proper waste management systems in urban slums.

Part VI: Conclusion

The Swachh Bharat Mission has been highly successful in improving sanitation outcomes across India, but there are regional disparities. Focused interventions in underperforming states and behavior change campaigns will be crucial for achieving the vision of a clean India by 2030.
